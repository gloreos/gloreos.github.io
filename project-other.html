// CONSUMER ONBOARDING

Problem

The Century21 mobile app required users to sign up in order to save a home, contact an agent, or perform other actions. However, once a user clicked on the call-to-action for a task, they would lose the context for what action they wanted to perform.

Solution

I changed the screen titles (e.g. Schedule a Tour) to indicate from where the user enters the flow. I also used the redesign as an opportunity to add hints of playfulness.

// STYLE GUIDE

As I came across questions regarding style and consistency, I consulted the product and marketing team—as well as outside resources such as Shopify Polaris and the MailChimp Style Guide—to establish a company standard. I covered a broad range of topics, from voice and tone to grammar conventions. I recorded these decisions in a working style guide, complete with tips and examples.

EXCERPT

What about tone? If our voice reflects our personality, tone reflects our personality in the context of a particular situation… Sometimes we're encouraging; other times we're careful. Our tone adapts to the situation.

Informative We're an information service at heart, so find opportunities to be informative rather than vague…

DON'T use marketing language as microcopy.

DO keep it simple.
