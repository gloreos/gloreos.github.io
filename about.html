About Me

My passion for design—as my passion for many things—must have begun with a book. Not a design book (not yet), but I’m sure it was some book. Maybe, If You Give a Mouse a Cookie or Diary of a Spider?

In a roundabout way, reading was my first step into design thinking. Beginning to see the world from another character’s perspective. A cab driver? A government agent? A mother of two? I loved to trace out—not the user’s journey—the hero’s journey.

My next immersion to design must have been the eleventh grade, when my English teacher taught my class to “gather the evidence” before writing the thesis. You’d think it’s that simple. But then at university, I learned, “there’s always more evidence.” You turn in the best draft you can.

These small lessons in reading and writing formed the foundation to my design career. I just hadn’t known it yet. When I was finally introduced to that one design book—The Design of Everyday Things?—I thought it was pretty neat. People like the author, Don Norman, were (kind of) doing what I was doing! They asked questions. Why can’t we open our doors? They gathered evidence. People struggle to open this kind of door and that sort of door. They wrote a thesis. We should make these kinds of doors.

Sounds fun. Why not try? And I jumped in.

I recently started as a part UX Writer/Researcher, and there’s a lot to learn. But I find myself falling into a nice rhythm that reminds me of the writing process.

I love stories, and strangely enough, I’ve grown into a love for writing essays. Somehow, that’s brought me here. To design. Hello, nice to meet you!
